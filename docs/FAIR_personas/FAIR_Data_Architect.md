## 	Description_of_role_of_	FAIR_Data_Architect
A role in an organization focusing on processes, models and policies that manages the generation, use and governance of data, metadata  and data standards in a FAIR compliant manner. Develops processes to deliver and maintain FAIR data and services

##	Persona_tasks_of_	FAIR_Data_Architect
A FAIR Architect in a large pharmaceutical organization is responsible for designing and maintaining the enterprise data architecture with a focus on FAIR compliance. This includes defining data models, metadata schemas, ontologies, and standards that ensure all relevant organizational data is findable, accessible, interoperable, and reusable. They develop governance policies, workflows, and pipelines to deliver FAIR data and services, often coordinating with Data Engineers, Clinical Data Managers, and FAIR Community Managers. Their work includes evaluating and implementing data platforms, standardizing data formats, establishing data lineage and provenance frameworks, and ensuring regulatory compliance while enabling cross-functional analytics, AI, and machine learning initiatives.		
		
		
##  	Issues_and_latent_gains_for_	FAIR_Data_Architect
###	Issues_for_	FAIR_Data_Architect
Issues: The Data  Architect faces challenges related to heterogeneity, scale, and complexity of pharma data. Data comes from multiple sources (lab equipment, imaging, "omics", electronic note books, clinical trials, real-world evidence) with inconsistent standards, metadata quality, and provenance. Integrating these sources into a coherent  (FAIR) framework while balancing regulatory, privacy, and operational requirements is complex. Additionally, driving organization-wide adoption of  good (FAIR) data practices requires alignment across multiple stakeholders, overcoming legacy systems, and managing trade-offs between technical feasibility, cost, and usability.. 		
###	Latent_gains_for_	FAIR_Data_Architect
Latent  gains: implementing FAIR principles would reduce these challenges and unlock efficiency, compliance, and reuse.		
		
		
## 	Description FAIR-benefits for	FAIR_Data_Architect
FAIR-aligned data facilitates the work of the data architect in the long run . Standardized metadata, ontologies, and governance frameworks reduce errors, streamline integration, and improve collaboration across departments. Data is consistently structured, discoverable, and of controllable quality across multiple sources, making it easier for teams to access and leverage information for analytics, regulatory submissions, and research.  By establishing clear data lineage, provenance, and documentation, FAIR practices enhance transparency and trust in data, support reproducibility, and enable efficient reuse for future projects. Ultimately, FAIR adoption allows the FAIR Architect to optimize data workflows, minimize duplication of effort, and create a scalable, sustainable data infrastructure that drives better decision-making across the organization.
