## 	Description of role 	Business_Owner
The Business Owner is an executive-level business role accountable for the value, performance, and strategic success of a defined business process, product, system, or dataset. As the designated authority within their domain, the Business Owner ensures that data and processes deliver measurable business value, meet defined KPIs, and align with organizational objectives and regulatory requirements.

In accordance with DAMA-DMBOK2, the Business Owner is responsible for defining business requirements, approving data standards and policies, and ensuring that data is managed as a strategic enterprise asset. They collaborate with Data Stewards, Architects, and IT teams to translate business needs into data capabilities, oversee the quality and fitness-for-purpose of data, and drive continuous improvement.

By championing sound data governance and FAIR-aligned practices, the Business Owner ensures that information assets are consistent, compliant, and reusable—enabling efficient decision-making, regulatory confidence, and sustained business value generation across the organization.

##	Persona tasks of	Business_Owner
The Business Owner performs a set of core tasks that ensure data, processes, and systems within their domain deliver measurable business value and align with corporate and regulatory expectations. Their tasks include defining business requirements and objectives for data and digital initiatives, ensuring they support strategic priorities. They evaluate and track business value and KPIs, measuring the performance and success of data-driven processes and products. The Business Owner also approves and enforces data policies, standards, and quality rules, guaranteeing that information is accurate, complete, and business-ready. They collaborate with Data Stewards, Data Architects, and IT teams to translate business needs into practical governance and data management solutions. Additionally, they prioritize and sponsor data initiatives, allocate resources, and resolve cross-functional challenges related to data ownership, access, and quality—driving continuous improvement and ensuring that data assets contribute directly to organizational goals.		
		
		
##  	Issues and latent gains for	Business_Owner
###	Issue for 	Business_Owner
Issues: Business Owners often face unclear accountability for data assets, leading to neglected stewardship and missed opportunities. Data is frequently treated as a by-product rather than a strategic asset, limiting its contribution to business value. Inconsistent metadata, poor provenance, and fragmented data quality management erode trust and make data unreliable for decision-making. Access policies are uneven and siloed, hindering operational agility, while weak interoperability and duplicate datasets prevent cross-domain insight generation. Additionally, limited literacy in FAIR principles and data governance frameworks leaves Business Owners unable to interpret metrics or demonstrate value. As a result, compliance efforts become reactive cost centers, and FAIR maturity remains disconnected from measurable business outcomes. Paradoxically, even FAIR initiatives aimed to improve the sitation  may be driven by IT or compliance functions without alignment to core business goals or KPIs, creating the perception of limited ROI. 


		
###	Latent gains for	Business_Owner
Latent  Gains:
Implementing FAIR principles establishes clear business data ownership and embeds stewardship as a core governance function. Data becomes an enterprise asset: traceable, high-quality, and aligned with business objectives. Standardized metadata, access protocols, and interoperable systems ensure consistency, trust, and agility across departments. FAIR maturity strengthens regulatory compliance, turning oversight into strategic advantage through transparent provenance and auditability. Empowered by reliable, well-documented data, Business Owners can link FAIR investments directly to performance indicators such as reduced time-to-insight, faster innovation cycles, and lower operational risk. With improved data literacy and measurable ROI, FAIR transforms data management from a technical exercise into a business enabler, driving sustained efficiency, compliance, and value creation across the organization."		
		
		
## 	Description of FAIR-benefits for	Business_Owner
For a Business Owner, adopting FAIR data principles delivers both strategic and operational value by turning data into a reliable, reusable business asset. Findable and Accessible data accelerate evidence-based decision-making and reduce time spent locating or validating information, improving responsiveness and time-to-market. Enhanced metadata and provenance increase trust, data quality, and regulatory compliance while reducing audit risk and operational errors. Interoperable data promote collaboration and cross-functional reporting, supporting integration during organizational changes or mergers. Through reusability, FAIR minimizes data duplication, lowers operational costs, and improves ROI on data investments. By making data machine-actionable, FAIR enables automation, AI, and innovation, enhancing forecasting, modeling, and digital transformation. Finally, improved traceability and lifecycle stewardship reinforce transparency, governance, and enterprise resilience. Together, these capabilities empower the Business Owner to maximize business value, ensure compliance, and drive sustainable growth.
